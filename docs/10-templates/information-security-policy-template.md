## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the organization's commitment to protecting information and operating an effective information security management system (ISMS).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- employees
- contractors
- temporary workers
- relevant suppliers
- systems, information, and processes within the ISMS scope

## 3. Policy statement

The organization shall protect information according to its value, sensitivity, legal requirements, business criticality, and risk.

Information security objectives include:

- preserving confidentiality
- preserving integrity
- preserving availability
- meeting legal, regulatory, contractual, and stakeholder requirements
- managing information security risk
- continually improving the ISMS

## 4. Principles

- security is risk-based
- responsibilities are assigned
- access is granted on least privilege
- information is classified and handled accordingly
- incidents are reported and managed
- suppliers are managed based on risk
- security is considered in projects and changes
- performance is monitored and reviewed

## 5. Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Top management | Approves policy and provides resources |
| ISMS manager | Coordinates the ISMS |
| Risk owners | Own and accept risks |
| Asset owners | Define protection needs |
| Control owners | Operate and evidence controls |
| All personnel | Follow policies and report incidents |

## 6. Exceptions

Exceptions must be documented, risk-assessed, approved, time-limited, and reviewed.

## 7. Violations

Violations may result in corrective action, disciplinary action, contract action, or legal action, depending on severity and applicable rules.

## 8. Review

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually and after significant changes.

## Usage guidance

Use this template to document **Information Security Policy**.

## Evidence to retain

Retain the approved record, source evidence, approval history, exceptions, and follow-up actions under the organization's retention and protection rules.

## Practical example

For one in-scope service, the owner completes the **Information Security Policy** record, links authoritative evidence, obtains review, and tracks rejected or incomplete items to closure.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This exact template is not an ISO requirement; it is guidance for recording **Information Security Policy** consistently. Controlled values, named owners, timestamps, approvals, and authoritative evidence improve assurance.
