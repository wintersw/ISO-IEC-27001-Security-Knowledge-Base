# Management Security Report Template

## 1. Executive summary

Summarize the most important risk changes, control failures, decisions, and actions in plain business language. State what management needs to decide rather than repeating the detailed sections.

## 2. Current risk posture

Describe material risks, changes since the previous report, residual-risk decisions, overdue treatments, and any uncertainty or incomplete coverage in the underlying data.

## 3. Current threat situation

Explain the threats relevant to the organization's assets and services, the source and confidence of the assessment, observed changes, and actions triggered by the analysis.

## 4. Security objective progress

Report each approved objective, target, current result, trend, accountable owner, forecast, and corrective action where progress is outside tolerance.

## 5. Major incidents and lessons learned

Summarize significant incidents, business impact, response effectiveness, recurring causes, lessons learned, and the status of verified corrective actions without exposing unnecessary sensitive detail.

## 6. Control assurance results

Report control coverage, tests performed, failures and exceptions, systemic issues, and whether management can rely on the evidence. Distinguish activity completion from tested effectiveness.

## 7. Audit findings and corrective actions

Show open and recently closed findings, severity, root cause, owner, due date, ageing, and effectiveness-review status. Highlight overdue or repeatedly reopened items.

## 8. Supplier and cloud risk

Summarize critical supplier changes, assurance results, incidents, concentration or exit risks, contract gaps, and remediation requiring management attention.

## 9. Resource needs

Connect requested people, skills, services, or funding to specific risks, objectives, obligations, and expected outcomes. State the consequence of deferral.

## 10. Prioritized recommendations

Rank recommendations using explicit risk, urgency, value, dependency, and effort criteria. Identify alternatives where management has a meaningful choice.

| Recommendation | Risk addressed | Benefit | Effort | Owner | Decision needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## 11. Decisions required

State each decision, accountable decision-maker, deadline, options considered, recommended choice, and the risk or consequence of no decision.

## 12. Action log

Record the action, owner, due date, status, evidence required for closure, escalation threshold, and effectiveness-review method.

## Usage guidance

Use this template to document **Management Security Report**. The owner defines its trigger and scope, uses authoritative sources, routes required approval, and tracks open items. Adapt the fields; this is guidance, not required ISO wording.

## Evidence to retain

Retain the approved record, source evidence, approval history, exceptions, and follow-up actions under the organization's retention and protection rules.

## Practical example

For one in-scope service, the owner completes the **Management Security Report** record, links authoritative evidence, obtains review, and tracks rejected or incomplete items to closure.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This exact template is not an ISO requirement; it is guidance for recording **Management Security Report** consistently. Controlled values, named owners, timestamps, approvals, and authoritative evidence improve assurance.
