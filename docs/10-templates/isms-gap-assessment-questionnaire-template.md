# ISMS Gap Assessment Questionnaire Template

> Based in part on the gap-analysis workbook in the [ISO 27001:2022 Toolkit](https://github.com/PehanIn/ISO-27001-2022-Toolkit), copyright (c) 2024 Pehan Gunasekara, MIT License. The source questionnaire has been replaced with a broader, evidence-oriented set of current management-system prompts and does not reproduce ISO text.

Use this as a structured starting point, not as a substitute for an authorized copy of ISO/IEC 27001:2022. Confirm clause requirements and amendments against the editions applicable to the organization.

## Assessment record

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Assessment ID and date |  |

| Rating scale | Not assessed / Absent / Initial / Defined / Operating / Effective |
| Evidence cutoff date |  |

For every prompt, record the rating, evidence reference, finding or gap, owner, priority, and due date. A policy without evidence of operation should not receive an “Operating” or “Effective” rating.

## Clause 4 — organizational context

| Ref. | Assessment prompt | Expected evidence examples |
|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | Are relevant internal and external issues identified, owned, and reviewed when circumstances change? | context register, strategic and threat inputs, review record |
| 4.2 | Are relevant interested parties and their information-security requirements identified and maintained? | interested-party and obligations registers |
| 4.3 | Does the scope state organizational, process, physical, technological, information, and supplier boundaries with defensible exclusions? | approved scope, boundary and dependency maps |
| 4.4 | Are the ISMS processes, interactions, responsibilities, and operating evidence defined and maintained? | process catalog, RACI, operating calendar, records |
| Amd 1:2024 | Has the organization determined whether climate change is a relevant issue and considered relevant interested-party requirements? | context decision and supporting rationale |

## Clause 5 — leadership

| Ref. | Assessment prompt | Expected evidence examples |
|---|---|---|
| 5.1 | Can top management demonstrate direction, integration, resourcing, support, and accountability for ISMS effectiveness? | decisions, budgets, communications, management reviews |
| 5.2 | Is the information security policy appropriate, approved, communicated, available, and maintained? | controlled policy, approval and communication records |
| 5.3 | Are security roles, authorities, accountabilities, and reporting paths assigned and understood? | role descriptions, RACI, interviews, governance minutes |

## Clause 6 — planning

| Ref. | Assessment prompt | Expected evidence examples |
|---|---|---|
| 6.1 | Are risks and opportunities arising from context and interested parties addressed through planned actions? | planning record, actions, owners, effectiveness review |
| 6.1.2 | Is the risk method repeatable, based on defined criteria, and capable of producing consistent results? | approved methodology, completed assessments, calibration records |
| 6.1.3 | Are treatment decisions linked to risks, control selection, the Statement of Applicability (SoA), owner approval, and residual-risk acceptance? | treatment plan, SoA, approvals, acceptance records |
| 6.2 | Are measurable security objectives assigned, monitored, communicated, updated, and supported by plans? | objectives register, measures, progress reviews |
| 6.3 | Are material ISMS changes planned with purpose, consequences, resources, responsibilities, and integrity considered? | change assessments, plans, approvals |

## Clause 7 — support

| Ref. | Assessment prompt | Expected evidence examples |
|---|---|---|
| 7.1 | Are people, time, funding, tools, and external services sufficient for intended ISMS outcomes? | resource plan, capacity and budget decisions |
| 7.2 | Is required competence defined, evaluated, developed, and evidenced for relevant roles? | competence matrix, qualifications, evaluations |
| 7.3 | Do personnel understand the policy, their contribution, and consequences of not following ISMS requirements? | interviews, campaigns, acknowledgements, observations |
| 7.4 | Are internal and external communications defined by topic, timing, audience, owner, and method? | communication plan, notifications, meeting records |
| 7.5 | Is documented information identified, approved, protected, versioned, distributed, retained, and disposed of under controlled rules? | document procedure, metadata, access and retention evidence |

## Clause 8 — operation

| Ref. | Assessment prompt | Expected evidence examples |
|---|---|---|
| 8.1 | Are planned ISMS processes operated under controlled criteria, including outsourced processes and planned or unintended changes? | procedures, tickets, service records, change reviews |
| 8.2 | Are risk assessments performed at planned intervals and after significant change using the approved method? | assessment schedule, event-triggered assessments |
| 8.3 | Are risk-treatment actions implemented, tracked, and evidenced? | treatment records, control evidence, overdue escalation |

## Clause 9 — performance evaluation

| Ref. | Assessment prompt | Expected evidence examples |
|---|---|---|
| 9.1 | Are monitoring and measurement subjects, methods, timing, responsibilities, analysis, evaluation, and retained results defined? | metric definitions, source data, dashboards, decisions |
| 9.2 | Does the audit program consider importance, changes, and previous results while preserving objectivity and reporting findings? | audit program, plans, competence and independence records |
| 9.3 | Does top management review required inputs and record decisions about suitability, adequacy, effectiveness, changes, resources, and improvement? | review pack, minutes, decision and action logs |

## Clause 10 — improvement

| Ref. | Assessment prompt | Expected evidence examples |
|---|---|---|
| 10.1 | Are improvement opportunities identified, prioritized, implemented, and checked for realized benefit? | improvement register, benefit and effectiveness reviews |
| 10.2 | Are nonconformities contained, corrected, cause-analyzed, remediated, checked for recurrence, and supported by retained evidence? | findings, root-cause records, corrective actions, verification |
