# Disaster Recovery Plan Template

## Scope

Identify the business processes, services, locations, systems, data, suppliers, and exclusions covered by the plan. State assumptions and dependencies that could affect recovery.

## Critical systems

List each system and service needed for recovery, its owner, dependencies, criticality, authoritative inventory reference, and current recovery capability.

## Recovery objectives

Record the approved recovery time objective, recovery point objective, maximum tolerable downtime, and minimum service level for each critical service. Explain any difference between the business need and current capability.

## Roles and contacts

Name the recovery lead, technical owners, business decision-makers, communications lead, supplier contacts, alternates, and escalation path. Store sensitive contact details with appropriate access controls.

## Recovery procedures

Describe ordered recovery steps, prerequisites, decision points, validation checks, rollback or alternative procedures, and the authority required to declare recovery complete.

## Communication plan

Define audiences, approval responsibilities, communication channels, fallback channels, message owners, notification triggers, and the source used for current contact details.

## Test schedule

Set risk-based test types and frequencies, scenarios, success criteria, participants, and an owner for remediation. Include technical restore tests as well as coordination exercises where relevant.

## Evidence and lessons learned

Retain test scope, execution records, timestamps, results, deviations, decisions, and corrective actions. Record what changed in the plan or recovery capability and verify that significant actions were effective.

## Usage guidance

Adapt this template to your organization, assign an owner, define retention, and store completed records in a controlled evidence location.

## Practical example

For one in-scope service, the owner completes the **Disaster Recovery Plan** record, links authoritative evidence, obtains review, and tracks rejected or incomplete items to closure.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This exact template is not an ISO requirement; it is guidance for recording **Disaster Recovery Plan** consistently. Controlled values, named owners, timestamps, approvals, and authoritative evidence improve assurance.
