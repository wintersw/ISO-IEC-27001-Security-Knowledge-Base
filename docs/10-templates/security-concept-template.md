# Security Concept Template

## 1. Purpose and scope

State the decision this security concept supports and identify the services, processes, information, systems, environments, users, suppliers, and lifecycle stages covered or excluded.

## 2. Business context

Explain the service purpose, business owners, users, critical outcomes, operating environment, assumptions, and risk appetite in language understandable to decision-makers.

## 3. Information assets and critical services

List the information and supporting services that require protection, their owners, classification or protection needs, criticality, locations, dependencies, and authoritative inventory references.

## 4. Legal, regulatory, contractual, and stakeholder requirements

Record applicable obligations in original summaries, identify their source and owner, and explain how each affects design, operation, evidence, or notification duties.

## 5. Security objectives

Define measurable confidentiality, integrity, availability, authenticity, accountability, privacy, resilience, or other relevant outcomes and how achievement will be tested.

## 6. Security organization and responsibilities

Assign accountable owners, operators, approvers, assurance roles, incident contacts, supplier responsibilities, and escalation routes. Address conflicts of interest and required separation of duties.

## 7. Protection needs / classification

Explain the criteria and decisions used to classify information and services. Record aggregation, dependency, legal, privacy, safety, and availability considerations rather than relying only on labels.

## 8. Risk assessment summary

Summarize material scenarios, affected assets, existing controls, likelihood and impact rationale, treatment decisions, risk owners, and links to the full risk record.

## 9. Selected security measures

| Measure | Risk addressed | Owner | Status | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## 10. Implementation plan

Sequence the selected measures with owners, dependencies, resources, acceptance criteria, evidence requirements, target dates, and decision gates. Identify temporary risk during transition.

## 11. Residual risks

Record risk remaining after planned measures, the acceptance authority and rationale, conditions of acceptance, review date, and triggers for reassessment.

## 12. Review and maintenance

Define the regular review frequency and triggers such as architecture change, new data use, supplier change, incident, vulnerability, legal change, or assurance finding.

## 13. Appendices

Attach or link detailed diagrams, inventories, data flows, risk records, test plans, evidence indexes, approvals, and supporting standards while keeping authoritative sources under document control.

## Usage guidance

Use this template to document **Security Concept**.

## Evidence to retain

Retain the approved record, source evidence, approval history, exceptions, and follow-up actions under the organization's retention and protection rules.

## Practical example

For one in-scope service, the owner completes the **Security Concept** record, links authoritative evidence, obtains review, and tracks rejected or incomplete items to closure.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This exact template is not an ISO requirement; it is guidance for recording **Security Concept** consistently. Controlled values, named owners, timestamps, approvals, and authoritative evidence improve assurance.
