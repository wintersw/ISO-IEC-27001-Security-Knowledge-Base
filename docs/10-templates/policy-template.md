# Policy Template

Tailor this structure to the policy's audience and risk. Policy statements should be mandatory, testable, and assigned to accountable roles; implementation detail that changes frequently is usually better placed in supporting standards or procedures.

## 1. Purpose

Explain the security or business outcome the policy establishes and the risks, obligations, or management decisions that make it necessary.

## 2. Scope

Define the people, processes, information, systems, locations, and third parties covered. State justified exclusions and how conflicts with other requirements are resolved.

## 3. Policy statements

Write clear, mandatory, testable rules. Identify the triggering condition or frequency where relevant and avoid implementation detail that belongs in a frequently changed standard or procedure.

## 4. Roles and responsibilities

Assign a policy owner, approver, operators, reviewers, and exception authority. Distinguish accountability from the roles that perform daily activities.

## 5. Exceptions

Define how exceptions are requested, risk-assessed, approved, time-limited, monitored, and reviewed. Require compensating controls and an expiry or remediation plan.

## 6. Compliance

Explain how adherence is monitored, how suspected violations are handled, and how legal, contractual, employment, and disciplinary considerations are respected.

## 7. Related documents

Link the supporting standards, procedures, controls, risk records, templates, and checklists needed to implement the policy without duplicating their content.

## 8. Review and maintenance

Set the regular review frequency and event-driven triggers such as material change, incident, audit finding, legal change, or control failure. Record approval and change history.

## Usage guidance

Use this template to document **Policy**. The owner defines its trigger and scope, uses authoritative sources, routes required approval, and tracks open items. Adapt the fields; this is guidance, not required ISO wording.

## Evidence to retain

Retain the approved record, source evidence, approval history, exceptions, and follow-up actions under the organization's retention and protection rules.

## Practical example

For one in-scope service, the owner completes the **Policy** record, links authoritative evidence, obtains review, and tracks rejected or incomplete items to closure.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This exact template is not an ISO requirement; it is guidance for recording **Policy** consistently. Controlled values, named owners, timestamps, approvals, and authoritative evidence improve assurance.
