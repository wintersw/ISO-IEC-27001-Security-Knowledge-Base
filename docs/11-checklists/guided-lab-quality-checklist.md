# Guided Lab Quality Checklist

- [ ] scenario is realistic
- [ ] learning objectives stated
- [ ] facts provided
- [ ] tasks are clear
- [ ] deliverables defined
- [ ] suggested answer included
- [ ] review questions included
- [ ] links to templates and chapters provided

## Evidence

Retain reviewer, date, findings, and improvement actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **Guided Lab Quality**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **Guided Lab Quality**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **Guided Lab Quality**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
