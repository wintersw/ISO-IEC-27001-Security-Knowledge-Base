# Continual Improvement Audit Evidence Checklist

- [ ] improvement register exists and is current
- [ ] improvement sources are recorded
- [ ] items are linked to risks, controls, findings, or incidents
- [ ] owners and due dates are assigned
- [ ] high-priority overdue items are escalated
- [ ] corrective actions include root-cause analysis
- [ ] effectiveness reviews are completed
- [ ] management review includes improvement status
- [ ] Statement of Applicability (SoA) and risk register are updated where needed
- [ ] repeat findings are analyzed

## Evidence

Retain the completed checklist with reviewer, date, decision, and follow-up actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **Continual Improvement Audit Evidence**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **Continual Improvement Audit Evidence**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **Continual Improvement Audit Evidence**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
