# Internal Audit Questionnaire

> Based in part on the internal-audit checklist and plan in the [ISO 27001:2022 Toolkit](https://github.com/PehanIn/ISO-27001-2022-Toolkit), copyright (c) 2024 Pehan Gunasekara, MIT License. Questions have been rewritten, expanded, and aligned with risk-based auditing and this project's evidence model.

Select questions that match the approved audit scope and criteria. Do not answer from documentation alone: corroborate with interviews, observation, system data, and representative samples.

## Governance and scope

- [ ] Do context, interested-party, scope, and dependency records agree with current operations?
- [ ] Can responsible people explain their authority, decisions, escalation paths, and evidence responsibilities?
- [ ] Are objectives and policies translated into operating processes rather than existing only as approved documents?
- [ ] Have material organizational, supplier, technology, legal, threat, or climate-relevance changes been evaluated?

## Risk management and SoA

- [ ] Is the approved risk method used consistently across selected samples?
- [ ] Are risk scenarios specific enough to support treatment and ownership decisions?
- [ ] Can each treatment decision be traced to actions, controls, the SoA, evidence, and residual-risk approval?
- [ ] Are SoA applicability justifications, implementation statuses, owners, and evidence current and internally consistent?
- [ ] Are overdue treatment actions and risks outside acceptance criteria escalated?

## Operational controls

- [ ] Do sampled control activities follow their defined frequency, population, criteria, and approval rules?
- [ ] Is control evidence complete, protected, attributable, and reproducible from authoritative sources?
- [ ] Are exceptions time-bound, risk-assessed, approved, monitored, and closed or renewed deliberately?
- [ ] Are supplier-operated controls assigned, monitored, and supported by contractual or assurance evidence?
- [ ] Do incidents, changes, vulnerabilities, continuity tests, and access reviews feed back into risk and control records?

## Competence, awareness, and communication

- [ ] Are competence requirements defined for sampled roles and supported by evaluation evidence?
- [ ] Can personnel explain relevant security responsibilities and reporting routes?
- [ ] Is awareness effectiveness evaluated using outcomes or behavior, not attendance alone?
- [ ] Are required internal and external communications timely, authorized, and retained where necessary?

## Measurement and management review

- [ ] Are selected metrics defined with owner, source, calculation, target, frequency, limitations, and decision threshold?
- [ ] Can reported values be reproduced from source data?
- [ ] Does management review receive complete, decision-ready inputs and record decisions, resources, owners, and due dates?
- [ ] Are previous management-review and audit actions followed through to verified closure?

## Nonconformity and improvement

- [ ] Are nonconformities corrected, cause-analyzed proportionately, and checked for similar issues elsewhere?
- [ ] Does effectiveness verification test whether recurrence risk was reduced rather than merely whether an action was completed?
- [ ] Are improvement opportunities prioritized, owned, measured, and reviewed for realized benefit?

## Evidence and sampling discipline

- [ ] Is the population defined before selecting samples?
- [ ] Is the selection method appropriate to risk, frequency, variability, and known exceptions?
- [ ] Are contradictory evidence and sampling limitations recorded?
- [ ] Are conclusions limited to what the evidence and sample support?
- [ ] Are findings factual, criteria-based, reproducible, and agreed for factual accuracy without compromising auditor independence?
