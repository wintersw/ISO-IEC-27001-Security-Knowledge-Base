# Risk Assessment Quality Checklist

- [ ] risk scenarios are specific and understandable
- [ ] asset or process owner is identified
- [ ] risk owner is accountable
- [ ] impact and likelihood criteria are applied consistently
- [ ] existing controls are listed
- [ ] treatment option is selected
- [ ] residual risk is evaluated
- [ ] risk acceptance is approved where needed
- [ ] treatment actions have owners and due dates
- [ ] risk is linked to Statement of Applicability (SoA) where relevant

## Evidence

Retain the completed checklist with reviewer, date, decision, and follow-up actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **Risk Assessment Quality**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **Risk Assessment Quality**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **Risk Assessment Quality**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
