# ITSM Evidence Quality Checklist
- [ ] record has owner
- [ ] affected service or asset identified
- [ ] approval visible where required
- [ ] timestamps retained
- [ ] security impact captured
- [ ] evidence attachments retained
- [ ] closure outcome documented
- [ ] linked records preserved

## Evidence

Retain the completed checklist with reviewer, date, decisions, and follow-up actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **ITSM Evidence Quality**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **ITSM Evidence Quality**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **ITSM Evidence Quality**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
