# Policy Quality Checklist

- [ ] policy purpose is clear
- [ ] scope is defined
- [ ] roles and responsibilities are included or referenced
- [ ] requirements are practical and enforceable
- [ ] exceptions process is defined
- [ ] violation handling is defined
- [ ] owner and approver are assigned
- [ ] review date is defined
- [ ] policy has been communicated
- [ ] acknowledgement is tracked where needed

## Evidence

Retain the completed checklist with reviewer, date, decision, and follow-up actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **Policy Quality**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **Policy Quality**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **Policy Quality**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
