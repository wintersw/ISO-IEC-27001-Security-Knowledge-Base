# Control Guidance Adaptation Checklist

Use this checklist when translating ISO/IEC 27002 or other generic guidance into an organizational control. It does not reproduce or replace the source guidance.

## Applicability and intended outcome

- [ ] The relevant risk, requirement, business need, and SoA decision are identified.
- [ ] The intended security outcome is written in organization-specific language.
- [ ] In-scope services, processes, information, technology, locations, people, and suppliers are explicit.
- [ ] Exclusions, assumptions, dependencies, and compensating measures are documented.
- [ ] Related controls and failure dependencies have been considered.

## Control design

- [ ] The preventive, detective, corrective, deterrent, or recovery role is understood.
- [ ] Trigger, frequency, population, method, decision criteria, and expected output are defined.
- [ ] Accountable owner, operator, reviewer, approver, and escalation authority are assigned.
- [ ] Required competence, access, tools, capacity, and supplier responsibilities are available.
- [ ] Exceptions and emergency operation have time limits, approval rules, monitoring, and closure criteria.
- [ ] Privacy, safety, legal, contractual, operational, and usability effects are evaluated where relevant.

## Lifecycle and integration

- [ ] The control is integrated into request, acquisition, design, build, test, release, operation, change, transfer, and retirement stages as applicable.
- [ ] Upstream inputs and downstream consumers are identified.
- [ ] Material changes, incidents, findings, threat changes, and failed tests trigger reassessment.
- [ ] Records, data, accounts, keys, components, suppliers, and evidence have retention and disposal rules.

## Evidence and measurement

- [ ] Design evidence shows why the control should achieve the intended outcome.
- [ ] Operating evidence is attributable, timestamped, complete, protected, and reproducible.
- [ ] The authoritative population and source systems are identified.
- [ ] Measures have definitions, calculation rules, owners, targets or thresholds, limitations, and decision responses.
- [ ] Testing covers both design and operation, including representative exceptions and failure cases.
- [ ] Effectiveness criteria address the intended outcome rather than activity completion alone.

## Approval and maintenance

- [ ] Residual risk and unresolved design limitations are visible to the authorized decision-maker.
- [ ] Procedures, training, contracts, SoA entries, evidence catalogs, and test plans are consistent.
- [ ] The control has an approval record, effective date, review schedule, and event-driven review triggers.
- [ ] Obsolete versions and evidence remain traceable under documented retention rules.
