# small or medium-sized enterprise (SME) Certification Decision Checklist

- [ ] customer requirement exists
- [ ] scope is realistic
- [ ] owners assigned
- [ ] risk process operating
- [ ] evidence available
- [ ] management committed
- [ ] cost and effort understood
- [ ] certification timing justified

## Evidence

Retain the completed checklist, reviewer, date, decisions, and follow-up actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **SME Certification Decision**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **SME Certification Decision**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **SME Certification Decision**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
