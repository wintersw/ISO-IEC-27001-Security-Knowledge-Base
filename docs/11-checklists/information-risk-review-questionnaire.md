# Information Risk Review Questionnaire

> Based in part on the information-risk checklist in the [ISO 27001:2022 Toolkit](https://github.com/PehanIn/ISO-27001-2022-Toolkit), copyright (c) 2024 Pehan Gunasekara, MIT License. It has been rewritten to be scenario-based, method-neutral, and evidence-oriented.

Record `Yes`, `Partial`, `No`, or `Not applicable` with evidence, rationale, owner, and action. A “Yes” answer requires evidence appropriate to the question.

## Scope and inputs

- [ ] Are the assessed service, process, information, technology, location, lifecycle stage, and supplier dependencies clearly bounded?
- [ ] Are accountable asset, service, process, and risk owners identified?
- [ ] Are authoritative asset, data-flow, dependency, vulnerability, incident, threat, legal, contractual, and audit inputs current?
- [ ] Have planned changes and plausible external changes been considered?

## Risk identification and analysis

- [ ] Are scenarios expressed as cause or threat exploiting a weakness, leading to a business consequence?
- [ ] Are confidentiality, integrity, availability, privacy, safety, financial, legal, contractual, operational, and reputational consequences considered where relevant?
- [ ] Does likelihood consider exposure, capability, frequency, control strength, and uncertainty rather than intuition alone?
- [ ] Is impact assessed against defined organizational criteria and realistic service dependencies?
- [ ] Are existing controls evaluated for actual design and operation before calculating residual risk?
- [ ] Are assumptions, data limitations, disagreements, and uncertainty visible?

## Evaluation and treatment

- [ ] Is each risk compared with approved acceptance criteria?
- [ ] Is the selected treatment—modify, avoid, share, or retain—documented with rationale?
- [ ] Are treatment actions specific, resourced, owned, dated, and linked to controls and the SoA?
- [ ] Are control dependencies and potential treatment side effects considered?
- [ ] Is residual risk reassessed after planned treatment and accepted by an authorized risk owner?
- [ ] Are overdue actions and risks above tolerance escalated through defined governance?

## Monitoring and change

- [ ] Does each risk have a review frequency and event-driven triggers?
- [ ] Are key risk indicators or other signals linked to decisions and escalation thresholds?
- [ ] Do incidents, near misses, audit findings, failed tests, supplier changes, and threat intelligence update relevant risks?
- [ ] Are closed or retired risks supported by evidence that the exposure ended or was transferred?
- [ ] Can management trace material risk changes, accepted exposure, and resource decisions over time?

## Review output

| Review item | Result | Evidence / rationale | Action or decision | Owner | Due / review date |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  | Yes / Partial / No / N/A |  |  |  |  |
