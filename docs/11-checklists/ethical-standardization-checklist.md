# Ethical Standardization Checklist

- [ ] purpose is clear
- [ ] affected stakeholders identified
- [ ] proportionality assessed
- [ ] privacy impact considered
- [ ] participation opportunity provided
- [ ] exceptions process defined
- [ ] sanctions are fair
- [ ] communication plan prepared
- [ ] review date defined

## Evidence

Retain the completed checklist, reviewer, date, decisions, and follow-up actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **Ethical Standardization**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **Ethical Standardization**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **Ethical Standardization**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
