# Cybersecurity Governance Review Checklist

- [ ] business objectives linked to cybersecurity outcomes
- [ ] risk appetite defined in operational terms
- [ ] decision rights documented
- [ ] high risks visible to accountable leaders
- [ ] management actions tracked
- [ ] supplier risk included
- [ ] assurance challenges optimistic reporting
- [ ] resource constraints escalated

## Evidence

Retain reviewer, date, findings, and improvement actions.

## How to use this checklist

Use this checklist for **Cybersecurity Governance Review**. Assign a reviewer, scope and deadline, and evidence for each answer. Failed items require an owned action, exception, or risk decision; a checked box alone is not evidence.

## Practical example

Before approving **Cybersecurity Governance Review**, the reviewer checks an authoritative source, assigns a failed item for remediation, and signs off only after resolution or formal acceptance is evidenced.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

This checklist is guidance, not prescribed ISO wording. Formal requirements depend on the scoped outcome supported by **Cybersecurity Governance Review**. Evidence retention and independent review of high-risk failures are good practice.
