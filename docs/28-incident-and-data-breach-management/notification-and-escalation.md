# Notification and Escalation

This page provides a generic workflow. It is not legal advice. Local legal, regulatory, contractual, and sector-specific rules must be reviewed.

## Decision workflow

1. confirm incident facts
2. assess affected data
3. assess affected parties
4. determine jurisdiction and obligations
5. determine contractual notification requirements
6. prepare internal management briefing
7. prepare external communications if required
8. record decision and rationale
9. track notification completion
10. retain evidence

## Escalation triggers

- personal data involved
- regulated data involved
- customer data involved
- critical service disruption
- supplier breach
- law enforcement involvement
- media risk
- high financial impact
- executive decision required

## Typical evidence

- approved policy, standard, procedure, or architecture record
- risk assessment or design review
- owner and role assignment
- implementation plan
- operating records
- monitoring records
- exception or waiver decisions
- test results
- audit records
- management review decisions

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **Notification and Escalation** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

A suspected customer-data exposure is triaged using this guidance. The response lead records facts and decisions, preserves evidence, assesses notification duties, and tracks corrective actions after containment.
