# Breach Impact Assessment

## Assessment factors

- data types involved
- sensitivity
- volume
- identifiability
- encryption or masking status
- affected individuals or entities
- misuse likelihood
- fraud risk
- safety risk
- legal or contractual duties
- containment status
- third-party involvement

## Output

The assessment should produce:

- breach classification
- notification recommendation
- containment actions
- evidence needs
- management briefing
- risk register update
- lessons learned actions

## Typical evidence

- approved policy, standard, procedure, or architecture record
- risk assessment or design review
- owner and role assignment
- implementation plan
- operating records
- monitoring records
- exception or waiver decisions
- test results
- audit records
- management review decisions

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **Breach Impact Assessment** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

A suspected customer-data exposure is triaged using this guidance. The response lead records facts and decisions, preserves evidence, assesses notification duties, and tracks corrective actions after containment.
