# Elevated Protection Needs

Some environments require security practices beyond baseline due to sensitivity, criticality, regulation, public interest, safety, or contractual obligations.

## When to consider elevated protection

- highly sensitive personal data
- critical infrastructure
- classified or regulated information
- high availability requirements
- high-value intellectual property
- safety-related processes
- public-sector obligations
- high contractual penalties

## Additional practices

### Target-group-specific security policies

Create tailored guidance for:

- administrators
- developers
- executives
- HR
- finance
- support staff
- remote workers
- suppliers
- incident responders

### Enhanced assurance

- more frequent internal audits
- independent control testing
- privileged access monitoring
- advanced logging and monitoring
- tabletop exercises
- red-team or penetration testing where appropriate

### Insurance review

Insurance may transfer some financial impact of residual risk. It does not replace controls.

Review:

- cyber insurance scope
- exclusions
- incident notification obligations
- alignment with current risk profile
- evidence obligations during claims

## Evidence

- elevated protection assessment
- target-group policies
- risk analysis
- insurance review
- enhanced control test results
- management approval


## Practical example

An organization uses this guidance to strengthen an existing ISO/IEC 27001 process with a clearly assigned security role, direct escalation, and practical operating records suited to its governance model.
