# Economic Use of Security Resources

Security resources should be allocated where they reduce the most relevant risk and support business objectives.

## Why this matters

Unbalanced investment can create a false sense of security. Expensive tools can fail if ownership, process, or basic controls are missing.

## Typical resource problems

- buying tools before defining processes
- overprotecting low-value systems
- underprotecting critical business processes
- ignoring staff workload
- failing to fund ongoing operation
- treating implementation cost as the only cost

## Resource planning fields

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Risk addressed | Why investment is needed |
| Control or measure | What will be implemented |
| Expected benefit | Risk reduction or compliance outcome |
| Implementation cost | Project effort |
| Operating cost | Ongoing people, tools, maintenance |
| Owner | Accountable role |
| Priority | Based on risk and business need |

## Best practices

- Prioritize by risk and criticality.
- Include operating effort, not only purchase cost.
- Review whether controls are actually used.
- Consider compensating controls.
- Document management decisions on resource constraints.
- Reassess investments when risks change.

## Evidence

- budget request
- risk treatment plan
- management decision
- cost-benefit analysis
- resource allocation record
- control effectiveness review
