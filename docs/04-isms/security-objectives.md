# Information Security Objectives

Information security objectives translate strategy and risk priorities into measurable outcomes.

## Good objective characteristics

A useful objective is:

- relevant to risk and business needs
- measurable
- assigned to an owner
- time-bound
- monitored
- reviewed by management

## Examples

| Objective | Measurement |
|---|---|
| Improve vulnerability response | 95% of critical vulnerabilities remediated within target |
| Strengthen access governance | 100% of privileged access reviewed quarterly |
| Improve resilience | Restore tests completed successfully for all critical backup sets |
| Improve incident readiness | Two tabletop exercises completed per year |

## Common mistakes

- Objectives that are impossible to measure.
- Objectives unrelated to risk.
- Objectives with no owner.
- Reporting activity without evaluating outcome.

## Evidence

- objective register
- measurement plan
- metric reports
- management review minutes
- action plans


## Practical example

The information security management system manager applies this guidance during the annual planning cycle, assigns accountable owners, connects the activity to risks and objectives, and schedules an effectiveness review.
