# User Awareness and Security Culture Surveys

Surveys help measure whether security awareness, communication, and culture are effective.

## Why surveys matter

Training completion alone does not prove understanding. Surveys can reveal whether employees know how to report incidents, understand policies, feel safe reporting mistakes, and believe security processes are usable.

## Security culture survey

Use a 1–5 agreement scale.

| Statement | Purpose |
|---|---|
| I know how to report a suspected security incident. | Incident reporting awareness |
| I understand my responsibility for protecting company information. | Role awareness |
| I know where to find security policies. | Policy communication |
| I feel comfortable reporting a mistake or concern. | Psychological safety |
| Security requirements are clear in my role. | Role-specific clarity |
| Security processes help me work safely. | Usability |
| I have received enough training for the information I handle. | Training effectiveness |

## Remote working survey

- I understand secure remote-working requirements.
- I know how to protect company devices outside the office.
- I know what to do if a device is lost or stolen.
- I use approved channels for storing and sharing work information.
- I understand the rules for working in public places.

## Developer secure coding survey

- I understand secure coding standards.
- I know how to report security defects.
- Security requirements are clear before development starts.
- Security testing results are understandable.
- I know how to handle secrets in code and pipelines.

## Control owner survey

- I understand which controls I own.
- I know what evidence is required.
- I know the review frequency.
- I understand how to report control exceptions.
- I know when to update the SoA or risk register.

## Best practices

- Keep surveys short.
- Include free-text feedback.
- Track trends over time.
- Share results with management.
- Convert findings into improvement actions.


## Practical example

After reaching 98% training completion, an ISMS manager runs the security culture survey and finds that only 60% of staff agree with "I know how to report a suspected security incident," and free-text comments describe the reporting form as hard to find. The team adds a one-click reporting button to the intranet and covers reporting in team briefings; the next survey cycle shows agreement rising to 85%, and the trend is presented at management review as evidence that awareness — not just training completion — improved.

## Evidence to retain

Retain records showing surveys are run and acted on, such as:

- survey questionnaires and anonymized response data per cycle
- trend analysis comparing cycles and target groups
- improvement actions derived from survey findings
- management reporting of survey results and follow-up outcomes
