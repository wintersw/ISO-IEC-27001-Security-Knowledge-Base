# Risk Owner Review Pack

Risk owners must understand and approve the risks they own. The ISMS team may facilitate risk management, but risk acceptance should remain with accountable business owners.

## Review objectives

A risk owner review should confirm:

- risk scenario remains accurate
- impact and likelihood remain valid
- treatment actions are progressing
- residual risk is acceptable
- assumptions remain true
- related incidents, findings, or changes are considered
- review date is updated

## Risk owner questions

1. Does the risk scenario still describe a realistic business risk?
2. Has the asset, process, supplier, or threat environment changed?
3. Are existing controls still operating?
4. Are treatment actions overdue?
5. Is residual risk still acceptable?
6. Are compensating controls still valid?
7. Should the risk be escalated to management?

## Review evidence

- reviewed risk register entry
- risk owner approval
- treatment action status
- updated residual-risk decision
- escalation record where applicable

## Common mistakes

- Security accepts risk without business owner approval.
- Risk owners approve entries they do not understand.
- Residual risk is accepted indefinitely.
- Treatment actions are not linked to real work.
