# Annual ISMS Operating Calendar

An ISMS should operate continuously. This calendar translates ISO/IEC 27001 activities into a practical yearly rhythm.

## Monthly activities

- Review new and changed risks.
- Review overdue risk treatment actions.
- Review open corrective actions.
- Review high-risk exceptions.
- Review security incidents and lessons learned.
- Review critical vulnerabilities and remediation status.
- Update evidence register.
- Review key KPI/KRI dashboard.

## Quarterly activities

- Conduct privileged access review.
- Review critical asset inventory.
- Review supplier changes and high-risk suppliers.
- Review control owner attestations.
- Review security objectives progress.
- Review policy exceptions and waivers.
- Conduct governance or steering committee review.
- Update SoA where controls or risks changed.

## Semi-annual activities

- Perform selected internal audits.
- Run incident tabletop exercise.
- Test disaster recovery or restore scenarios.
- Review security awareness effectiveness.
- Review risk scoring calibration.
- Review climate-related relevance and resilience assumptions where applicable.

## Annual activities

- Full ISMS context review.
- Interested-party requirements review.
- ISMS scope review.
- Risk methodology review.
- Full or risk-based risk assessment refresh.
- Management review.
- Internal audit program review.
- Policy framework review.
- Certification/surveillance readiness review.

## Calendar template

| Period | Activity | Owner | Evidence | Output |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly | Risk and treatment review | ISMS Manager | Risk register export | Updated actions |
| Quarterly | Privileged access review | IAM Owner | Access review report | Remediation tickets |
| Semi-annual | Incident tabletop | Security Operations | Exercise report | Lessons learned |
| Annual | Management review | Top Management | Minutes and action log | Decisions and improvements |

## Best practices

- Put recurring ISMS activities on the calendar at the start of the year.
- Assign owners and evidence expectations before the activity occurs.
- Use management review to evaluate trends, not just status.
- Use the calendar as an audit-ready operating record.
