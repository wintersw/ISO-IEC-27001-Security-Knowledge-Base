# Cybersecurity Roles and Authorities

Role descriptions often list duties but fail to define authority. Governance requires both.

## Decision-right examples

| Decision | Typical accountable role |
|---|---|
| accept critical residual risk | executive risk owner |
| approve information security policy | top management |
| approve security architecture exception | architecture/security authority |
| approve supplier with unresolved high risk | accountable business owner plus risk authority |
| approve emergency privileged access | designated operational authority |
| approve notification after a breach | legal/privacy/executive authority according to process |

## Practical example

A control owner may operate quarterly access reviews, but the business data owner must decide whether access is still justified. The ISMS manager coordinates the process, internal audit independently tests it, and management resolves resource or accountability failures.

## Best practices

- Define authority separately from responsibility.
- Identify deputies for critical roles.
- Avoid assigning independent assurance to the same person who owns the control.
- Document escalation paths for unresolved risk and overdue action.
- Review roles after reorganizations, acquisitions, and outsourcing.
