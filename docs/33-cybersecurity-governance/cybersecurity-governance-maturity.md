# Cybersecurity Governance Maturity

## Maturity levels

### Level 1 — Reactive

Decisions are made after incidents. Ownership is unclear and reporting is inconsistent.

### Level 2 — Defined

Policies, roles, and governance forums exist, but decisions may not be consistently implemented.

### Level 3 — Operating

Risk, metrics, supplier issues, and assurance results are routinely reviewed. Decisions have owners and deadlines.

### Level 4 — Measured

Governance evaluates trends, risk appetite, control effectiveness, and business outcomes.

### Level 5 — Adaptive

Governance uses scenario analysis, predictive indicators, and lessons learned to change strategy before major failures occur.

## Assessment questions

- Are security decisions linked to business objectives?
- Are overdue risk decisions visible?
- Are governance actions tracked?
- Are supplier risks included?
- Does assurance challenge optimistic reporting?
- Are management decisions verified for implementation?

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **Cybersecurity Governance Maturity** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

Executives use this guidance during a risk-oversight meeting to set direction, assign authority, challenge performance and risk information, and record decisions requiring management action.

## Evidence to retain

Retain records showing both design decisions and actual operation, such as:

- approved strategy or governance decision
- authority and accountability record
- risk and performance reporting
- oversight minutes and tracked actions

Intent documents are insufficient on their own; retain scoped operating records, approvals, exceptions, and verified follow-up.
