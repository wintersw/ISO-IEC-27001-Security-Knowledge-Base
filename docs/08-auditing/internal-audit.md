# Internal Audit

Internal audit evaluates whether the information security management system (ISMS) conforms to planned arrangements and ISO/IEC 27001 requirements, and whether it is effectively implemented and maintained.

## Audit program

An audit program should consider scope, risk, previous findings, process importance, control maturity, auditor competence, independence, and audit frequency.

## Audit methods

- document review
- interviews
- sampling records
- observing processes
- reperforming selected steps
- tracing evidence from risk to control to operation

## Security-team preparation

Do not create artificial audit evidence. Operate the process normally and make sure evidence is retrievable. Auditors value consistency more than polished presentations.

## Detailed explanation

Internal audit provides independent, systematic evaluation of whether the ISMS conforms to planned arrangements and operates effectively. It should be risk-based and cover the full ISMS over a defined program.

### Example

An annual program prioritizes identity lifecycle, vulnerability management, supplier security, and incident response because they contain high risks and previous findings. The auditor samples records, interviews owners, observes workflows, and tests remediation.

### Best practices

- define an audit universe
- prioritize high-risk and changed areas
- preserve auditor independence
- use clear criteria
- test design and operation
- sample complete populations
- record positive evidence and failures
- classify findings consistently
- track corrective action
- verify effectiveness

### Common mistakes

Audits become document checks, auditors review their own work, low-risk areas are repeatedly audited while high-risk processes are ignored, and findings are closed without effectiveness testing.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **Internal Audit** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

An internal auditor samples a recently completed activity, traces it from the governing requirement to the operating record, checks exceptions, and confirms that identified weaknesses resulted in effective corrective action.

## Evidence to retain

Retain records showing both design decisions and actual operation, such as:

- audit scope, criteria, and sampling rationale
- source evidence and interview notes
- findings and agreed actions
- follow-up evidence confirming effectiveness

Intent documents are insufficient on their own; retain scoped operating records, approvals, exceptions, and verified follow-up.
