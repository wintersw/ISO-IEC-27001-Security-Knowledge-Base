# Internal Audit

Internal audit evaluates whether the ISMS conforms to planned arrangements and ISO/IEC 27001 requirements, and whether it is effectively implemented and maintained.

## Audit program

An audit program should consider scope, risk, previous findings, process importance, control maturity, auditor competence, independence, and audit frequency.

## Audit methods

- document review
- interviews
- sampling records
- observing processes
- reperforming selected steps
- tracing evidence from risk to control to operation

## Security-team preparation

Do not create artificial audit evidence. Operate the process normally and make sure evidence is retrievable. Auditors value consistency more than polished presentations.

## Detailed explanation

Internal audit provides independent, systematic evaluation of whether the ISMS conforms to planned arrangements and operates effectively. It should be risk-based and cover the full ISMS over a defined program.

### Example

An annual program prioritizes identity lifecycle, vulnerability management, supplier security, and incident response because they contain high risks and previous findings. The auditor samples records, interviews owners, observes workflows, and tests remediation.

### Best practices

- define an audit universe
- prioritize high-risk and changed areas
- preserve auditor independence
- use clear criteria
- test design and operation
- sample complete populations
- record positive evidence and failures
- classify findings consistently
- track corrective action
- verify effectiveness

### Common mistakes

Audits become document checks, auditors review their own work, low-risk areas are repeatedly audited while high-risk processes are ignored, and findings are closed without effectiveness testing.
