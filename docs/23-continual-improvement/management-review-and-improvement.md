# Management Review and Improvement

Management review is where improvement becomes visible to leadership and where decisions are made on priorities, resources, objectives, and risk acceptance.

## Improvement inputs for management review

- open improvement backlog
- overdue high-priority actions
- repeat audit findings
- corrective-action effectiveness
- incident lessons learned
- control assurance failures
- user survey themes
- supplier issues
- risk treatment blockers
- resource constraints
- proposed objective changes

## Management decisions

Management may decide to:

- approve resources
- accept residual risk
- change objectives
- revise priorities
- escalate overdue actions
- change policy direction
- approve control redesign
- accept timeline changes
- request further assurance

## Reporting format

| Topic | Status | Decision needed | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| High-priority improvement overdue | Red | Approve additional resource or accept delay risk | Top management |
| Repeat access review finding | Amber | Approve automation project | identity and access management (IAM) / Security |
| Supplier incident lessons learned | Amber | Approve new supplier review requirement | Procurement / Security |

## Evidence

- management review pack
- decision log
- action tracker
- improvement register
- dashboard
- resource approval


## Practical example

A recurring control failure is entered into the improvement process. The owner analyzes the cause, implements a proportionate change, and verifies after an agreed period that the failure has not recurred.
