# information security management system (ISMS) Continual Improvement Lifecycle

The continual improvement lifecycle is a repeatable process for turning lessons, weaknesses, opportunities, and decisions into measurable improvement.

## Lifecycle stages

### 1. Collect improvement inputs

Inputs can come from:

- internal audits
- external audits
- incidents
- near misses
- vulnerabilities
- risk reviews
- supplier reviews
- management review
- control testing
- employee feedback
- security culture surveys
- threat intelligence
- legal or contractual changes
- cloud and technology changes
- customer security reviews

Do not create a separate process for each source. Use a single improvement register or backlog.

### 2. Evaluate significance

Not every suggestion needs formal treatment. Evaluate whether the item affects:

- risk exposure
- control effectiveness
- legal or contractual compliance
- audit readiness
- customer trust
- operational resilience
- security culture
- cost or efficiency
- management objectives

### 3. Record the improvement item

Record enough detail to make the item actionable.

Minimum fields:

- improvement ID
- source
- description
- related risk/control/clause
- owner
- priority
- target date
- required resources
- status
- effectiveness review method

### 4. Prioritize

Prioritize by risk and value, not by who speaks loudest.

Consider:

- severity of risk
- urgency
- regulatory or contractual driver
- affected assets
- implementation effort
- dependency on other work
- customer impact
- likelihood of repeat failure
- management priority

### 5. Approve or assign

Some improvements can be assigned directly. Others require management decision, budget, risk acceptance, or scope change.

### 6. Implement

Implementation may involve:

- changing a policy
- improving a procedure
- configuring a technical control
- adding monitoring
- assigning ownership
- training staff
- updating a supplier contract
- revising the risk register
- updating the SoA

### 7. Verify effectiveness

An improvement is not complete merely because an action was closed. Verify that it addressed the issue.

Examples:

- retest failed control
- sample new access reviews
- check trend improvement
- verify incident recurrence reduced
- audit updated process
- survey affected users
- confirm evidence quality improved

### 8. Update ISMS records

Update affected documents and records:

- risk register
- treatment plan
- SoA
- procedures
- evidence register
- metrics dashboard
- audit program
- training material
- management review action log

### 9. Report to management review

Management review should see improvement trends, not only open action lists.

Report:

- major improvement actions
- overdue actions
- repeat findings
- effectiveness results
- resource needs
- changes to risk posture
- decisions required

## Evidence

- improvement register
- action tracker
- risk register updates
- SoA updates
- corrective action records
- control test results
- management review minutes
- effectiveness review records

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **ISMS Continual Improvement Lifecycle** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

A recurring control failure is entered into the improvement process. The owner analyzes the cause, implements a proportionate change, and verifies after an agreed period that the failure has not recurred.
