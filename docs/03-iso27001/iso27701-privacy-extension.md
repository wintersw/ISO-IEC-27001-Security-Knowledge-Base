# ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Privacy Information Management System

ISO/IEC 27701:2025 specifies requirements and guidance for a privacy information management system (PIMS). Unlike the withdrawn 2019 edition, the 2025 second edition is a standalone management-system standard. An organization can implement and certify the PIMS independently or integrate it with an ISO/IEC 27001 information security management system (ISMS).

## Why it matters

Many security controls protect personal data, but privacy also requires governance over lawful processing, data subject rights, controller/processor roles, retention, purpose limitation, transparency, and privacy impact.

## Practical integration

- Extend asset inventory into a data inventory.
- Link data processing to legal basis and purpose.
- Add privacy risks to the risk register.
- Include privacy requirements in supplier reviews.
- Align incident response with breach notification obligations.
- Include privacy metrics in management review.

## Evidence

- data inventory
- privacy notices
- DPIAs
- processing records
- supplier privacy terms
- data subject request records

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** ISO/IEC 27701:2025 contains PIMS requirements for organizations acting as PII controllers, processors, or both. Its requirements apply when an organization chooses or is obligated to conform to that standard; ISO/IEC 27001 does not itself require adoption of ISO/IEC 27701.
- **Implementation guidance:** Identify the organisation's role as a PII controller or processor, document the legal basis for processing, define data-subject-rights procedures, and integrate privacy impact assessments into the ISMS risk assessment process.
- **Best practice:** Maintain a single integrated management system rather than running the ISMS and PIMS as separate programmes with duplicate governance structures.

## Practical example

The data protection officer maps the existing ISMS and privacy processes against ISO/IEC 27701:2025 requirements for the organization's controller and processor roles. Gaps are identified in data-subject-request handling, purpose and legal-basis records, and privacy-by-design evidence. The privacy team assigns actions and decides which governance and assurance processes can be shared with the ISMS.

## Edition note and authoritative source

ISO/IEC 27701:2019 was withdrawn on publication of the standalone second edition. Validate implementation and certification decisions against [ISO/IEC 27701:2025](https://www.iso.org/standard/27701?browse=tc) and an authorized copy of the standard.
