# ISO/IEC 27701 Privacy Information Management

ISO/IEC 27701 extends ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO/IEC 27002 toward privacy information management.

## Why it matters

Many security controls protect personal data, but privacy also requires governance over lawful processing, data subject rights, controller/processor roles, retention, purpose limitation, transparency, and privacy impact.

## Practical integration

- Extend asset inventory into a data inventory.
- Link data processing to legal basis and purpose.
- Add privacy risks to the risk register.
- Include privacy requirements in supplier reviews.
- Align incident response with breach notification obligations.
- Include privacy metrics in management review.

## Evidence

- data inventory
- privacy notices
- DPIAs
- processing records
- supplier privacy terms
- data subject request records

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **ISO/IEC 27701 Privacy Information Management** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

An organization preparing for certification maps this topic to its scoped processes, identifies the responsible role, and checks that documented arrangements agree with day-to-day operation before an internal audit.
