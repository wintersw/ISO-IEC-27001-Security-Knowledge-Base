# ISO/IEC 27036 Supplier Relationship Security

ISO/IEC 27036 provides guidance for information security in supplier relationships.

## Why it matters

Modern organizations depend on software as a service (SaaS) providers, cloud platforms, managed service providers, contractors, and outsourced development teams. Supplier weaknesses can become organizational incidents.

## Practical supplier lifecycle

1. Identify supplier need.
2. Classify supplier risk.
3. Perform due diligence.
4. Include security requirements in contract.
5. Onboard securely.
6. Monitor performance.
7. Review changes.
8. Offboard and confirm data return or deletion.

## Evidence

- supplier inventory
- supplier risk rating
- due diligence assessment
- contract security clauses
- monitoring records
- offboarding evidence

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **ISO/IEC 27036 Supplier Relationship Security** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

An organization preparing for certification maps this topic to its scoped processes, identifies the responsible role, and checks that documented arrangements agree with day-to-day operation before an internal audit.
