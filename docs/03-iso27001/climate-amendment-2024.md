# 2024 Climate-Action Amendment

ISO/IEC 27001:2022/Amd 1:2024 adds climate-action changes to the management-system context requirements.

## Practical interpretation

When analyzing organizational context, determine whether climate change is relevant to the information security management system (ISMS). Interested parties may also have climate-related requirements.

This does not mean every organization needs a standalone climate-security program. It means the decision must be considered and defensible.

## Security examples

Climate-related issues may affect:

- data-center cooling and power resilience
- flood, wildfire, heat, and severe-weather exposure
- supplier and logistics continuity
- workforce location and remote-working dependency
- insurance and customer requirements
- long-term infrastructure capacity

## Evidence to retain

- context-analysis entry showing the consideration
- rationale for relevance or non-relevance
- related risks, dependencies, and treatment where applicable
- management-review discussion when material

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** This chapter explains **ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Amendment 1:2024** without reproducing standard text. Determine formal obligations from the applicable clauses, scope, risk treatment, Statement of Applicability, and binding legal or contractual requirements.
- **Implementation guidance:** Adapt the described roles, frequency, workflow, and evidence to the organization.
- **Best practice:** Enhancements are optional unless adopted through policy, contract, or risk treatment.

## Practical example

An organization preparing for certification maps this topic to its scoped processes, identifies the responsible role, and checks that documented arrangements agree with day-to-day operation before an internal audit.
