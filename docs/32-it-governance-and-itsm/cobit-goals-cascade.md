# COBIT Goals Cascade for Security

The goals cascade prevents security from becoming disconnected technical activity. It translates stakeholder needs into enterprise goals, alignment goals, governance and management objectives, operational practices, and measurable outcomes.

## Example cascade

A board-level need is:

> Maintain customer trust and reliable digital services.

This can become the following chain:

1. **Enterprise goal:** reliable and secure customer service.
2. **Alignment goal:** managed digital risk and service availability.
3. **Governance objective:** evaluate risk, direct investment, and monitor performance.
4. **Management objective:** manage security, service continuity, suppliers, and changes.
5. **Operational controls:** MFA, vulnerability management, backup testing, supplier assurance.
6. **Metrics:** critical service recovery tests, high-risk changes, account takeover incidents, overdue supplier findings.

## Why the cascade matters

Without a cascade, teams may optimize control completion without improving the business outcome. For example, reporting 100% patch-policy compliance has limited meaning if critical customer services still contain unowned legacy assets.

## Best practices

- Start with stakeholder and business outcomes.
- Keep the chain traceable from board objectives to operational evidence.
- Use the cascade to justify security investment.
- Revisit alignment when strategy or services change.
- Avoid creating a metric that cannot trigger a decision.
