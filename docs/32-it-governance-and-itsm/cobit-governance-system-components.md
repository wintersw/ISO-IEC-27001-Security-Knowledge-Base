# COBIT Governance System Components

A governance system is stronger than a set of processes. COBIT describes components that must work together: principles and policies, processes, organizational structures, information, culture and behavior, people and competencies, and services/infrastructure/applications.

## ISMS example: vulnerability management

### Policies and procedures

Define remediation targets, exceptions, risk approval, and verification.

### Processes

Discover, validate, prioritize, remediate, verify, and report.

### Organizational structures

Security operations, service owners, change authority, risk owners, and governance forum.

### Information

Asset inventory, scan results, threat intelligence, change records, exceptions, and metrics.

### Culture and behavior

Teams report weaknesses and do not hide overdue exposure.

### People and competencies

Analysts understand exploitability; service teams can remediate safely.

### Services and technology

Scanners, ticketing, CMDB, patch platforms, monitoring, and reporting.

## Lesson

If one component is weak, the process can fail. A scanner cannot compensate for missing asset ownership, and a policy cannot compensate for no remediation capacity.
