# People Security, Awareness, and Culture

People security aligns capability, access, incentives, and accountability across the employment and contractor lifecycle. Awareness is not a yearly presentation: it is a program that makes secure behavior understandable, practicable, and safe to report.

## Lifecycle controls

- Define screening that is lawful, proportionate, and relevant to the role.
- Put security responsibilities, confidentiality, and acceptable use into agreements and role descriptions.
- Give induction before access and role-based learning before sensitive work.
- Coordinate role changes with access review, knowledge transfer, and segregation-of-duties checks.
- Revoke access and recover assets promptly at departure while preserving continuing obligations.
- Provide trusted channels for reporting mistakes, suspicious behavior, and policy pressure without retaliation.

Insider risk includes malicious, negligent, and compromised people. Combine technical signals with human review, privacy safeguards, due process, and clear escalation; do not equate unusual behavior with wrongdoing.

## Program design and evidence

Use role and risk to select learning: developers need secure coding, finance teams need payment-fraud scenarios, administrators need privileged-access practice, and leaders need decision exercises. Measure reporting, comprehension, behavior, and control outcomes—not attendance alone.

Evidence includes role matrices, screening decisions, agreements, onboarding and offboarding records, learning completion and effectiveness results, reporting metrics, access reviews, and documented investigations.

## Practical example

A finance team rehearses a supplier bank-change scenario. Staff verify the request through an independently sourced channel, report the message, and receive rapid feedback. Management tracks report quality and prevented loss rather than shaming clicks.

## Common mistakes

- using identical training for every role
- measuring completion as proof of changed behavior
- punishing good-faith reporting and thereby suppressing early warning
- monitoring people without a defined purpose, legal basis, access controls, or review
