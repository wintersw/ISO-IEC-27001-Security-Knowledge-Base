# ISO 27001 and ISO 22301

ISO 22301 focuses on business continuity management. ISO/IEC 27001 focuses on information security management. They overlap where information systems support continuity and resilience.

## Common integration points

- business impact analysis
- recovery objectives
- information and communication technology (ICT) readiness
- incident and crisis management
- supplier continuity
- backup and recovery testing
- management review
- continual improvement

## Shared process, distinct outcomes

| Integrated activity | Information-security focus | Business-continuity focus |
|---|---|---|
| Context and scope | information, systems, and interested-party security needs | priority products/services and disruption context |
| Risk and impact analysis | confidentiality, integrity, and availability risk | disruption impact and time-based recovery priorities |
| Response arrangements | security-event and incident handling | command, communications, continuity, and recovery procedures |
| Exercises and tests | control and ICT recovery effectiveness | capability to continue and recover priority activities |
| Management review | ISMS suitability and effectiveness | business continuity management system suitability and effectiveness |

One integrated process may produce evidence for both systems, but each standard's scope, required inputs, objectives, and documented information must remain traceable.

## Practical example

A restore exercise verifies A.8.13 backup effectiveness and supports an ICT recovery objective. A wider continuity exercise still tests manual workarounds, people, premises, communications, dependencies, and the recovery of the priority business activity.
