# ISO 27001 and ISO/IEC 42001

ISO/IEC 42001 specifies a certifiable Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). It shares the Annex SL high-level structure with ISO/IEC 27001, which makes the two straightforward to integrate when an organization both secures information and governs AI systems.

## Practical relationship

| Management-system element | ISO/IEC 27001 | ISO/IEC 42001 |
|---|---|---|
| Context and interested parties | Clause 4 | Clause 4 (adds AI-specific stakeholders and impacts) |
| Leadership and policy | Clause 5 | Clause 5 (AI policy and responsible-AI objectives) |
| Planning and risk | Clause 6, Annex A controls | Clause 6 plus AI risk and AI system impact assessment |
| Support and operation | Clauses 7–8 | Clauses 7–8 (data, model, and lifecycle management) |
| Performance evaluation | Clause 9 | Clause 9 |
| Improvement | Clause 10 | Clause 10 |

## Where they meet

- **Shared structure:** Both use Annex SL, so context, leadership, planning, support, operation, evaluation, and improvement align clause by clause.
- **Distinct risk lenses:** ISO/IEC 27001 focuses on risk to information; ISO/IEC 42001 adds risk to and from AI systems, including impacts on individuals and society.
- **Complementary controls:** The Annex A information-security controls protect the data, models, and pipelines that AI governance depends on. See [AI Governance and Security](../38-ai-governance-and-security/index.md).

## Best practice

Integrate rather than duplicate: run a single management system with combined context, leadership, risk, and improvement processes, and add AI-specific controls (impact assessment, data governance, model lifecycle, transparency) on top of the ISMS. Keep AI risk decisions traceable to the same risk methodology used for information security.
