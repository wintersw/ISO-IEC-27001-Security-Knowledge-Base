# Framework Mappings

Mappings help organizations reuse evidence and understand how different frameworks relate. They should not be treated as automatic compliance.

## Included mappings

- [NIST Cybersecurity Framework](nist-csf.md)
- [CIS Controls](cis-controls.md)
- [SOC 2](soc2.md)
- [General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)](gdpr.md)
- [ISO 22301](iso22301.md)
- [OWASP](owasp.md)

## Mapping method

1. Record the exact framework editions, scope, systems, and reporting period.
2. Map requirements or outcomes to the organization's implemented controls—not only to Annex A control numbers.
3. Classify each relationship as full, partial, supporting, or no match, and explain the rationale.
4. Identify obligations with no counterpart and assign an owner.
5. Reuse evidence only when its population, period, ownership, and test method satisfy both purposes.
6. Have a competent owner approve the mapping and review it after edition, scope, control, or regulatory change.

A relationship table is a navigation aid, not proof of conformity. Similar wording does not establish equivalent scope, intent, implementation, or assurance.
