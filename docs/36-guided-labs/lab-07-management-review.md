# Lab 7 — Conduct Management Review

## Scenario

The information security management system (ISMS) dashboard shows:

- three high risks overdue for treatment
- one repeat audit finding
- critical vulnerability remediation at 82% within target
- one supplier incident
- security awareness completion at 98%
- restore-test success at 75%
- two management decisions overdue

## Tasks

1. Prepare an executive summary.
2. Identify required decisions.
3. Distinguish information from action.
4. Draft management-review outputs.
5. Define follow-up evidence.

## Suggested analysis

The 98% awareness figure is not the highest-priority issue. Restore-test success at 75% and overdue high risks may threaten critical services. The repeat audit finding suggests ineffective root-cause analysis. Management should decide resources, priorities, risk acceptance, and accountability.

## Suggested decisions

- fund recovery remediation
- assign executive owner for overdue high risks
- require effectiveness review for repeat finding
- review supplier incident impact
- close or reassign overdue governance actions

## Review questions

- Does the meeting evaluate effectiveness?
- Are decisions specific?
- Do actions have owners and dates?
- Are resource needs documented?


## Evidence to retain

Retain records showing both design decisions and actual operation, such as:

- completed lab output
- assumptions and decision rationale
- review feedback
- revised result and lessons learned

Intent documents are insufficient on their own; retain scoped operating records, approvals, exceptions, and verified follow-up.
