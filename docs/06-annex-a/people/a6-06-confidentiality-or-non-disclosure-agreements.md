# A.6.6 Confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements

## Overview

Confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements define which information must be protected, permitted use, disclosure conditions, duration, return or destruction, and consequences. Terms should match applicable law and the relationship's actual risk.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.6.6 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.6.6 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A specialist supplier signs an agreement covering customer data and architecture before receiving design material. The owner reviews the terms when the scope expands and records return or secure deletion at contract end.

## Evidence to retain

- approved policy, standard, or procedure
- owner and responsibility record
- operating records from the relevant workflow
- exception and risk-acceptance records
- control test or audit evidence
