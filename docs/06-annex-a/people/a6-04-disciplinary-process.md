# A.6.4 Disciplinary process

## Overview

A disciplinary process gives the organization a fair, lawful, and consistent way to address deliberate or negligent security violations. It should align with employment rules and consider evidence, intent, severity, recurrence, and due process.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.6.4 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **disciplinary process**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.6.4 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **disciplinary process** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

After repeated unauthorized sharing despite prior instruction, human resources and management review verified facts against published rules, apply the approved process consistently, restrict access where justified, and retain the decision securely.

## Evidence to retain

- approved policy, standard, or procedure
- owner and responsibility record
- operating records from the relevant workflow
- exception and risk-acceptance records
- control test or audit evidence
