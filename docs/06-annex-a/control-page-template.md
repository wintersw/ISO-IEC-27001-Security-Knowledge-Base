# Annex A Control Page Template

Use this structure for every control page. When drafting a page:

- explain concepts in plain language before introducing specialist terminology;
- distinguish ISO requirements from implementation guidance and optional best practice;
- include at least one practical example;
- identify the evidence the organization should retain;
- link to related clauses, controls, templates, and checklists instead of duplicating content;
- summarize and contextualize ISO requirements without reproducing copyrighted standard text; and
- use vendor-neutral language unless a named product is clearly identified as an example.

```markdown
# A.x.x Control Name

## Overview
Explain the control in plain language before introducing jargon or formal terminology.

## Purpose
Describe why the control exists and what risk, requirement, or business need it addresses.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice
Summarize the formal requirement in original language; do not reproduce text from the standard. Clearly label and distinguish:

- **ISO requirement:** what the organization is required to achieve;
- **Implementation guidance:** practical ways the organization can meet the requirement; and
- **Best practice:** optional measures that may improve effectiveness or assurance.

## Practical implementation
Describe how the control is implemented in practice, including roles, triggers, and operating expectations. (Optional for simple controls; include when operational detail adds value.)

## Practical example
Include a realistic example for a small organization, an enterprise, or a cloud/software as a service (SaaS) context.

## Evidence to retain
State what evidence should be retained to show that the control was designed and operated effectively.

## Common mistakes
Describe typical failures or weak implementations.

## Auditor questions
List questions an auditor might ask about design, operation, and evidence.

## Checklist
Provide a short checklist of the main implementation and evidence steps.

## Related controls, clauses, templates, and checklists
Link to related clauses, controls, templates, and checklists rather than repeating content.
```
