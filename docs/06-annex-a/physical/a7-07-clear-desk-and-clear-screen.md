# A.7.7 Clear desk and clear screen

## Overview

Clear-desk and clear-screen practices reduce exposure when information or devices are unattended. Requirements should reflect location, sensitivity, shared workspaces, visitors, printing, locking, and secure disposal.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.7.7 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **clear desk and clear screen**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.7.7 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **clear desk and clear screen** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

At the end of a shared-office shift, confidential notes are stored in a locked cabinet, printouts are collected, removable media is secured, and devices lock automatically after a short period of inactivity.

## Evidence to retain

- site risk assessment
- access records
- visitor records
- inspection or maintenance records
- incident and corrective-action records
