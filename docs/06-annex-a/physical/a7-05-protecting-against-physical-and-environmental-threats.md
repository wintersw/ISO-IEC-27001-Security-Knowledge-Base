# A.7.5 Protecting against physical and environmental threats

## Overview

Facilities and equipment can be harmed by fire, water, temperature, power, civil disturbance, and other environmental or physical threats. Site-specific assessment should drive prevention, detection, response, and recovery safeguards.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.7.5 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **protecting against physical and environmental threats**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.7.5 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **protecting against physical and environmental threats** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A basement communications room is found exposed to flood risk. The organization raises equipment, installs water detection, relocates backups, documents shutdown responsibility, and tests alerts and recovery arrangements.

## Evidence to retain

- site risk assessment
- access records
- visitor records
- inspection or maintenance records
- incident and corrective-action records
