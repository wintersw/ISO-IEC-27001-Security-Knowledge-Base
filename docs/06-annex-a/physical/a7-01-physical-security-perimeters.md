# A.7.1 Physical security perimeters

## Overview

Physical security perimeters define boundaries where protection increases around people, information, and equipment. Walls, doors, reception, barriers, and monitoring should match what is inside and how the space is used.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.7.1 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **physical security perimeters**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.7.1 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **physical security perimeters** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

An office places public meeting rooms outside the controlled boundary and server and records rooms inside it. A site review verifies doors, walls, emergency exits, shared-building risks, visitor routes, and after-hours arrangements.

## Evidence to retain

- site risk assessment
- access records
- visitor records
- inspection or maintenance records
- incident and corrective-action records
