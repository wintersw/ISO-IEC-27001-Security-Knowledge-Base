# A.7.8 Equipment siting and protection

## Overview

Equipment placement and protection should reduce theft, observation, tampering, environmental damage, and accidental disruption. The organization should consider public access, utilities, heat, water, dust, vibration, and maintenance needs.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.7.8 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **equipment siting and protection**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.7.8 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **equipment siting and protection** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A network cabinet is moved away from a public corridor and water pipe, locked, ventilated, and connected to monitored power protection. Cabling and indicators cannot be observed or reached by unescorted visitors.

## Evidence to retain

- site risk assessment
- access records
- visitor records
- inspection or maintenance records
- incident and corrective-action records
