# A.7.2 Physical entry

## Overview

Physical entry controls allow authorized people into protected areas and keep a reliable account of visitors and exceptions. Identification, authorization, badges, keys, escorts, logs, and access removal form one lifecycle.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.7.2 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **physical entry**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.7.2 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **physical entry** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A visitor is pre-approved, verifies identity at reception, receives a time-limited badge, is escorted in restricted areas, and returns the badge on exit. Failed badge returns and forced-door alarms are investigated.

## Evidence to retain

- site risk assessment
- access records
- visitor records
- inspection or maintenance records
- incident and corrective-action records
