# A.7.3 Securing offices, rooms and facilities

## Overview

Rooms and facilities need safeguards appropriate to the information, equipment, and activities they contain. Security includes layout, visibility, locking, occupancy, emergency access, and avoiding signs that reveal sensitive use.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.7.3 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **securing offices, rooms and facilities**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.7.3 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **securing offices, rooms and facilities** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A finance records room has controlled entry, no public-facing signage, locked cabinets, a clear emergency-access procedure, and periodic checks that stored materials and authorized-user lists remain current.

## Evidence to retain

- site risk assessment
- access records
- visitor records
- inspection or maintenance records
- incident and corrective-action records
