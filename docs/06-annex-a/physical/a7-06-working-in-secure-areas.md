# A.7.6 Working in secure areas

## Overview

Secure areas require operating rules in addition to locked doors. Authorized activities, escorts, recording devices, unattended work, emergency actions, maintenance, and removal of materials should be controlled and understood.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.7.6 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **working in secure areas**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.7.6 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **working in secure areas** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

Engineers entering a restricted lab use named badges, leave personal recording devices outside, log removable media, follow two-person maintenance rules, and reconcile tools and materials before leaving.

## Evidence to retain

- site risk assessment
- access records
- visitor records
- inspection or maintenance records
- incident and corrective-action records
