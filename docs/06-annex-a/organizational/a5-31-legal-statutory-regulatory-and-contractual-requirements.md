# A.5.31 Legal, statutory, regulatory and contractual requirements

## Overview

The organization needs a maintained view of legal, regulatory, and contractual security obligations and how they apply. Each obligation should have an owner, interpretation, implementation link, evidence, and change trigger.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.5.31 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **legal, statutory, regulatory and contractual requirements**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.5.31 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **legal, statutory, regulatory and contractual requirements** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A compliance owner records a new breach-notification term, maps it to the incident process, updates decision and communication templates, trains responders, and verifies the change during the next exercise.

## Evidence to retain

- approved policy, standard, or procedure
- owner and responsibility record
- operating records from the relevant workflow
- exception and risk-acceptance records
- control test or audit evidence
