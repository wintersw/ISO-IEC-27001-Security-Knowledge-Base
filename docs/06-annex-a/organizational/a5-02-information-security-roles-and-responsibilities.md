# A.5.2 Information security roles and responsibilities

## Overview

Security responsibilities need named owners and clear authority. People should know who makes decisions, who performs security work, who reviews it independently, and where unresolved issues are escalated.

## Purpose

This control ensures that information security responsibilities are defined, assigned, and communicated so that every security task has a named owner and people understand what is expected of them. When roles are ambiguous, security tasks fall through the cracks and accountability cannot be established.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.5.2 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **information security roles and responsibilities** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A 40-person company creates a responsibility matrix covering risk acceptance, access approval, incident command, supplier review, and internal audit. Named deputies cover absences, and role changes trigger a review of assigned duties and access.

## Evidence to retain

- approved policy, standard, or procedure
- owner and responsibility record
- operating records from the relevant workflow
- exception and risk-acceptance records
- control test or audit evidence

## Common mistakes

- Security responsibilities are vaguely assigned to "management" or "IT" without naming specific individuals or roles.
- Role descriptions focus on titles without defining security-specific duties, decision authority, or escalation paths.
- When people change roles or leave, their security responsibilities are not reassigned — creating orphaned duties.
- Deputy or backup arrangements for critical security roles are not defined.

## Auditor questions

- Where are information security roles and responsibilities documented, and how are they communicated?
- Who is responsible for each major security domain (risk, access, incident, compliance, supplier security)?
- How are security responsibilities reassigned when someone changes role or leaves the organization?
- Show evidence that role holders are aware of and have accepted their responsibilities.

## Checklist

- [ ] security roles and responsibilities documented
- [ ] role holders named and responsibilities accepted
- [ ] decision authority and escalation paths defined
- [ ] deputy/backup arrangements for critical roles in place
- [ ] responsibilities reassigned on role change or departure
- [ ] role descriptions reviewed and updated periodically
