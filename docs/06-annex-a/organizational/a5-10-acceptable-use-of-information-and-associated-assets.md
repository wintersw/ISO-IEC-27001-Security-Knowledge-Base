# A.5.10 Acceptable use of information and other associated assets

## Overview

Acceptable-use rules explain how people may and may not use information and associated assets. Effective rules cover handling, storage, sharing, personal use, monitoring, return, and consequences in language appropriate to the audience.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.5.10 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **acceptable use of information and other associated assets**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.5.10 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **acceptable use of information and other associated assets** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A developer is allowed limited personal use of a company laptop but may not upload source code or customer data to unapproved online services. The onboarding acknowledgement links to approved transfer and exception processes.

## Evidence to retain

- approved policy, standard, or procedure
- owner and responsibility record
- operating records from the relevant workflow
- exception and risk-acceptance records
- control test or audit evidence
