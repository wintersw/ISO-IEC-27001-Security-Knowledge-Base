# A.5.10 Acceptable use of information and other associated assets

## Overview

Acceptable-use rules explain how people may and may not use information and associated assets. Effective rules cover handling, storage, sharing, personal use, monitoring, return, and consequences in language appropriate to the audience.

## Purpose

This control ensures that rules for acceptable use of information and assets are defined, documented, communicated, and enforced — so that personnel understand what is permitted, what is prohibited, and what consequences follow misuse. Without clear rules, violations are contested and enforcement is inconsistent.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.5.10 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **acceptable use of information and other associated assets** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

A developer is allowed limited personal use of a company laptop but may not upload source code or customer data to unapproved online services. The onboarding acknowledgement links to approved transfer and exception processes.

## Evidence to retain

- approved policy, standard, or procedure
- owner and responsibility record
- operating records from the relevant workflow
- exception and risk-acceptance records
- control test or audit evidence

## Common mistakes

- The acceptable use policy is buried in a handbook that no one reads — there is no acknowledgment or reinforcement.
- Rules are too vague ("use assets responsibly") to be enforceable or too granular to be practical.
- Personal use is either completely banned (and ignored) or completely unregulated — no middle ground.
- Consequences for violations are stated but never applied — the policy has no teeth.

## Auditor questions

- Where is the acceptable use policy documented, and how is it communicated to all personnel?
- How do personnel acknowledge that they have read and understood the rules?
- How are violations detected and what consequences have been applied?
- Show evidence that the policy is reviewed and updated to reflect current technology and working practices.

## Checklist

- [ ] acceptable use policy documented and approved
- [ ] policy communicated to all personnel
- [ ] acknowledgment of understanding obtained
- [ ] personal use boundaries clearly defined
- [ ] violation detection and enforcement process in place
- [ ] policy reviewed and updated periodically
