# A.5.19 Information security in supplier relationships

## Overview

Supplier relationships can expose information and services beyond the organization's direct control. Supplier security should be proportionate to access, dependency, data sensitivity, substitutability, and the effect of supplier failure.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.5.19 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **information security in supplier relationships**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.5.19 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **information security in supplier relationships** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical implementation

### Measures that support decisions

- high-risk suppliers reviewed on time
- supplier findings overdue
- critical suppliers without exit plan
- supplier incidents

## Practical example

Before selecting a payroll provider, the owner evaluates data access, subcontractors, resilience, incident history, and exit feasibility, records residual risk, and sets assurance activities based on the provider's criticality.

## Evidence to retain

- supplier tiering decision
- due-diligence assessment
- contract security clauses
- service review record
- incident/change notification and exit evidence
