# A.5.20 Information security in supplier agreements

## Overview

Supplier agreements translate security and business needs into enforceable responsibilities. Requirements should cover the service lifecycle, including access, incidents, evidence, subcontracting, change, continuity, return or deletion, and exit.

## Purpose

This control ensures that relevant security requirements are included in supplier agreements, addressing topics such as access, data protection, incidents, assurance, subcontracting, change, and exit according to risk. Requirements should be clear, appropriately enforceable, and capable of verification; a generic confidentiality clause alone may not address the relationship's security needs.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.5.20 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **information security in supplier agreements** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical implementation

### Measures that support decisions

- high-risk suppliers reviewed on time
- supplier findings overdue
- critical suppliers without exit plan
- supplier incidents

## Practical example

A software-as-a-service contract defines breach notification, data locations, access controls, assurance reports, subcontractor conditions, recovery targets, secure deletion, and evidence the customer can request during the term.

## Evidence to retain

- supplier tiering decision
- due-diligence assessment
- contract security clauses
- service review record
- incident/change notification and exit evidence

## Common mistakes

- Agreements reference security in vague terms — "Supplier will maintain appropriate security" — with no specifics.
- Data location and subcontracting restrictions are omitted — the supplier can move data anywhere or subcontract without notice.
- Incident notification timelines are undefined — the supplier can delay breach notification indefinitely.
- Exit and data return/deletion obligations are missing — the organization cannot retrieve or ensure deletion of its data.

## Auditor questions

- Where are security requirements for suppliers documented, and how are they incorporated into contracts?
- What specific security obligations are included — incident notification, data location, audit rights, subcontracting?
- How are exit and data return/deletion requirements addressed in agreements?
- Show a recent supplier agreement that includes measurable security requirements.

## Checklist

- [ ] security requirements schedule defined for supplier agreements
- [ ] incident notification timelines specified
- [ ] data location and subcontracting restrictions included
- [ ] audit and assurance rights defined
- [ ] exit, data return, and deletion obligations specified
- [ ] agreement reviewed when services or risk changes
