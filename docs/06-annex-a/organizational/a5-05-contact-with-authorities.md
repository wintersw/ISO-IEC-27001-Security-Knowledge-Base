# A.5.5 Contact with authorities

## Overview

The organization should know which public authorities may need to be contacted, by whom, and under which conditions. Prepared contact and escalation arrangements reduce delay and inconsistent communication during incidents or legal events.

## Purpose

The purpose of A.5.5 is to reduce the likelihood or impact of failures related to **contact with authorities**. A well-designed control makes the expected outcome, accountability, operating trigger, exception path, and assurance method clear enough to be repeated and tested.

## ISO requirement, implementation guidance, and best practice

- **ISO requirement:** Annex A controls are not automatically mandatory. Determine necessary controls through risk treatment, record applicability in the Statement of Applicability, and implement A.5.5 when selected or otherwise committed to.
- **Implementation guidance:** Define **contact with authorities** through its owner, scope, trigger, workflow, exceptions, and evidence.
- **Best practice:** Embed it in normal work, test operation and effectiveness, and review it after material change or failure.

## Practical example

The incident plan names the roles authorized to contact the data-protection authority, law enforcement, and sector regulator. During a ransomware event, the incident lead records the notification decision, approver, time, and information shared.

## Evidence to retain

- approved policy, standard, or procedure
- owner and responsibility record
- operating records from the relevant workflow
- exception and risk-acceptance records
- control test or audit evidence
