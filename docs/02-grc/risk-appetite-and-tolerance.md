# Risk Appetite and Risk Tolerance

## Risk appetite

Risk appetite is the amount and type of risk the organization is willing to pursue or retain to achieve objectives.

## Risk tolerance

Risk tolerance defines acceptable variation around appetite, often expressed as thresholds.

## Examples

| Area | Appetite / tolerance example |
|---|---|
| Critical vulnerabilities | No critical internet-facing vulnerability remains open beyond target without approved exception |
| Privileged access | No standing privileged access without documented justification |
| Recovery | Critical services must meet approved recovery objectives |
| Supplier risk | High-risk suppliers require assessment before contract approval |

## Why this matters

Risk appetite prevents every risk decision from becoming a debate. It gives risk owners and security teams a common decision framework.

## Evidence

- approved risk appetite statement
- thresholds and escalation criteria
- residual risk approvals
- management review records
