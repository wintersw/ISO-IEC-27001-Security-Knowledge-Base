# Governance, Risk, and Compliance

GRC connects security activities with business objectives, legal obligations, accountability, and assurance.

## Governance

Governance defines who makes decisions, who is accountable, what rules apply, and how performance is overseen.

## Risk

Risk management identifies uncertainty that could affect objectives and determines what actions are appropriate.

## Compliance

Compliance demonstrates that obligations are met. Obligations may come from laws, contracts, internal policies, standards, customer requirements, or certification schemes.

## Why GRC matters to security teams

Without GRC, security teams often become reactive tool operators. With GRC, security work is prioritized, justified, measured, and accountable.
