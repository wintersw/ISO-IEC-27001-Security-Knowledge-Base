# FAQ

## Do we need to implement all Annex A controls?

No. Annex A controls are selected based on risk treatment and other requirements. The Statement of Applicability must justify inclusion and exclusion.

## Is ISO 27001 only for IT?

No. ISO 27001 covers people, processes, technology, physical security, suppliers, legal obligations, governance, and continual improvement.

## Can a small company implement ISO 27001?

Yes. The information security management system (ISMS) should be proportional to size, complexity, risk, and business context.

## Is certification mandatory?

Certification is not mandatory under the standard itself. It may be required by customers, contracts, regulators, or market expectations.

## Is ISO 27001 the same as ISO 27002?

No. ISO 27001 defines certifiable ISMS requirements. ISO 27002 provides control implementation guidance.

## What is the most important document?

There is no single most important document, but the scope, risk methodology, risk register, risk treatment plan, SoA, policy, internal audit records, and management review records are central.

## What do auditors usually look for?

Auditors look for conformity, implementation, evidence, consistency, effectiveness, and continual improvement.
