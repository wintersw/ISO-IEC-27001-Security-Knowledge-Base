# Certification and Scope Assurance

A certificate is useful only when its scope covers the relevant services, locations, processes, and information assets.

## Why scope assurance matters

Customers, regulators, and partners often ask for ISO/IEC 27001 certification. The certificate alone may not answer whether the specific service, product, location, or process they rely on is included.

## Scope assurance questions

- Which legal entity is certified?
- Which locations are included?
- Which services and business processes are included?
- Which cloud platforms and suppliers support the scoped services?
- Which exclusions exist?
- Are customer-facing services included?
- Are development, operations, support, and incident response included?
- Does the SoA cover controls relevant to the service?
- Does the evidence cover the requested period?

## Common pitfalls

- certificate exists but the requested service is outside scope
- scope statement is too vague
- outsourced processes are not clearly described
- shared services are relied upon but not controlled
- SoA exclusions are not justified
- control evidence exists only for part of the scope

## Certification readiness evidence

- approved scope statement
- scope boundary diagram
- interested-party register
- SoA
- risk register
- audit program
- management review minutes
- evidence register
- control owner list
- certification scope assurance record

## Practical example

A prospective customer asks a SaaS vendor for its ISO/IEC 27001 certificate before signing. Working through the scope assurance questions, the customer's assessor finds the certificate covers the vendor's corporate IT entity but excludes the data center operations subsidiary that actually hosts the service. The vendor is asked to provide the SoA, the scope boundary diagram, and supplier controls covering the subsidiary — turning a misleading "we are certified" claim into a precise picture of what assurance actually exists.

## Evidence to retain

Retain records demonstrating scope validity, such as:

- approved scope statement and boundary diagram matching the certificate
- justifications for SoA exclusions
- completed scope assurance records for customer or regulator inquiries
- evidence that outsourced and shared services supporting the scope are controlled
