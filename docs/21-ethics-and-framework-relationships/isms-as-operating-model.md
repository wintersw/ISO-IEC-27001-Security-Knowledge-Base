# ISMS as an Operating Model

An ISMS is the operating model for managing information security. It coordinates rules, processes, people, technology, evidence, and improvement.

## ISMS building blocks

- context and requirements
- scope
- leadership and responsibilities
- risk management
- security objectives
- policy framework
- control selection
- control operation
- awareness and competence
- incident management
- monitoring and measurement
- internal audit
- management review
- continual improvement

## Cultural perspective

A sustainable ISMS becomes part of daily work. Security is not only a policy or toolset; it becomes a shared way of making decisions.

## Practical maturity levels

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| Documented | Policies and processes exist |
| Operating | Processes are used consistently |
| Evidenced | Operation produces reliable records |
| Measured | Performance and risk are monitored |
| Integrated | Security is part of business and service management |
| Improving | Feedback drives visible improvements |

## Evidence

- ISMS calendar
- governance records
- risk register
- SoA
- evidence catalog
- metrics
- improvement backlog
